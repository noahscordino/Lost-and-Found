How we value your clothing

We keep it simple. Grade your bag, weigh it, and get an honest estimate before we even arrive. No per-item haggling — one clear offer for the whole collection.

Cash offers based on weight + quality grade
Grade A, B, or C — then weigh your bag. That's it.
How it works
Three steps from bag to offer.
1
Grade your bag
Look at the brands and condition of most items in your bag. Use the grade guide below to pick A, B, or C.
2
Weigh it
Pop it on your bathroom scales. A full black rubbish bag of clothing is usually around 5–8 kg.
3
Get your estimate
Enter the weight and grade below. We confirm the final offer when we arrive and see everything in person.

Which grade is your bag?

$5–6 / kg

Strong: Nike collab / vintage, Adidas trefoil, New Balance, Champion USA, Tommy Hilfiger 90s–00s, Nautica, Lacoste, North Face, Kathmandu
Condition

Excellent — worn 1–3 times, no fading or marks

Tip: pull out any Arc'teryx / Stone Island jackets, vintage Levi's 501s, or Supreme / Palace pieces and mention them when you book — these are worth assessing separately.
Grade B — mixed bag
$3–4 / kg
Brands
Mid-range: Country Road, Witchery, Zara, H&M trend pieces, Uniqlo linen / cashmere, Glassons, Cotton On — near-new only
Surf / skate / vintage: Billabong, Rip Curl, Quiksilver, Volcom, Thrasher, Santa Cruz, unbranded vintage pieces — label not required

Mix of good and fair across the bag
No stains, broken zips, or structural damage
Surf, skate, and vintage pieces sit in Grade B even without a visible label — don't downgrade them just because the brand isn't obvious.
Grade C — low value / donation
$1–2 / kg or free donation
Brands
Low-value: Kmart, Target, Postie, unbranded (non-vintage), no label
Any brand in poor condition — stains, damage, or heavy wear
Kids clothing — any brand, accepted as donation only
Condition
Poor — visible stains, broken zips, heavy pilling, or structural damage
Majority of bag is everyday fast-fashion in average or worse condition
